Canada child benefit

Apply

When to apply

You should apply for the Canada child benefit as soon as any of the following situations happen:

  • your child is born
  • a child starts to live with you, or returns to live with you after a temporary custody with someone else
  • you begin, end or change a shared custody arrangement
  • you get temporary custody of a child
  • you or your spouse or common-law partner starts to meet the eligibility conditions under Who can apply

If you apply more than 11 months after you became eligible, you will need to provide additional documents.

How to apply

  • Through birth registration

    Processing time: you should receive your payment within 8 weeks.

    You can apply for the Canada child benefit when you register the birth of your newborn. In most cases, the registration is done by paper at the hospital or birthing center. Residents of Ontario, Quebec and British Columbia also have the option to complete the registration process online.

    Be sure to give your consent and provide your social insurance number in order for the Vital Statistics Agency to securely share the birth registration information with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). If you do not give your consent, your information will not be sent to the CRA, and you will need to apply for the Canada child benefit separately.

    • Select your province or territory for information on how to register a birth.

      You will be redirected to the provincial or territorial website.

      • Saskatchewan
      • New Brunswick
      • Newfoundland and Labrador
      • Alberta
      • Manitoba
      • Ontario
      • Quebec
      • Prince Edward Island
      • British Columbia
      • Nova Scotia
      • Northwest Territories

      Currently, this service is not available in the territories of Yukon and Nunavut.

  • Online through My CRA Account

    Processing time: you should receive your payment within 8 weeks.

    If you haven’t already registered the birth of your newborn, you can apply for the Canada child benefit using your provincial or territorial birth registration process.

    Apply for the Canada child benefit with My Account (your personal CRA account):

    1. Log in to My CRA Account
    2. Go to “Apply for child benefits”
    3. Confirm your contact information, your marital status and your citizenship
    4. Add your child’s name, gender, date and place of birth, and caregiver information
    5. Review and submit your application
  • By mail

    Processing time: you should receive your payment within 11 weeks.

    1. Fill out and sign Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application
    2. Mail the form to the CRA to your tax center
    3. Include any additional documents needed for the situations described below

When you need to provide additional documents

  • Male parent is primarily responsible for the child

    If there is a female parent in the family, and you want the male parent to receive the payments, you must:

    1. Include a letter from the female parent stating that the male parent is primarily responsible for the care of all the children in the household
    2. Mail the letter and Form RC66 to your tax centre

    A letter is not required if both parents living with the child are of the same sex.

  • Child that was born outside Canada

    You must provide proof of birth with your application if the CRA has never paid benefits for this child.

    1. Provide proof of birth
    2. Mail the proof of birth and Form RC66 to your tax centre
  • Applying for a period that started more than 11 months ago

    If you are applying for a child that started living with you more than 11 months ago, you must:

    1. Provide supporting documents for
      1. Your citizenship status (1 of the following)
        • Canadian birth certificate
        • Canadian passport
        • permanent residence card
        • immigration status in Canada for you and your spouse or common-law partner
      2. Proof that you live in Canada (at least 3 different documents type)
        • lease agreement
        • rent receipt
        • household bill (gas, electricity, cable television, telephone)
        • driver's license, vehicle registration, or car insurance
        • membership in social or professional organizations
      3. Proof of birth for each child you are applying for
      4. Proof that you are primarily responsible for the care and upbringing of the child (at least 3 different document types):
        • a letter from the nursery or school authority showing the contact information on file for the child
        • a copy of the child's report card, showing the contact information on file for the child
        • a copy of the child's school registration or enrolment document, or information or emergency contact sheet, signed and certified by the school
        • if your child is being home-schooled, a letter from the school board or appropriate educational authority confirming that you have a home school arrangement
        • a registration form or a receipt from an activity or club the child is enrolled in
        • a complete and signed court order, decree, or separation agreement; the document must explain the type of custody arrangement you have (for example, shared custody) and clearly show the living arrangements for the child
        • any other document showing that the child lived with you during the period being reviewed
    2. Mail all supporting documents and Form RC66 to your tax centre.
  • You or your spouse or common-law partner are newcomers or returning residents to Canada

    If you are:

    • a newcomer or returned as a resident of Canada in the last two years
    • a new Canadian citizen within the last 12 months
    • a permanent resident
    • a protected person (refugee)
    • a temporary resident who has lived in Canada for the previous 18 months
    • not a Canadian citizen, but are an indigenous person who meets the definition of "Indian" under the Indian Act

    You must:

    1. Fill out and sign Schedule RC66SCH, Status in Canada/Statement of Income
    2. Mail Schedule RC66SCH and Form RC66 to your tax centre.
