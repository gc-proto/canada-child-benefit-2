Canada Child Benefit: Apply

  • Overview
  • Eligibility
  • Apply
  • How much you can expect
  • Payment dates

You must do your taxes every year to avoid delays or interruption in the payment of your Canada child benefit (CCB) – even if you didn’t earn any income. If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they also have to do their taxes every year. Only one parent can apply on behalf of all the children in the household.

If you haven’t already registered the birth of your newborn, you can apply for the CCB using your provincial or territorial birth registration process.

Apply for the CCB with My Account (Your personal CRA account)

  1. Log in to My Account
  2. Go to “Apply for child benefits”
  3. Confirm your contact information, your marital status and your citizenship
  4. Add your child’s name, gender, date and place of birth, and caregiver information
  5. Review and submit your application

Other ways to apply

Provincial or territorial birth registration process

You can apply for the CCB when you register the birth of your newborn. In most cases, the registration is done by paper at the hospital or birthing center. Residents of Ontario, Quebec and British Columbia also have the option to complete the registration process online.

All you have to do is ensure you give your consent and provide your social insurance number to allow your Vital Statistics Agency securely to share the information from your birth registration with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). If you do not give your consent, your information will not be sent to the CRA, you will need to apply for the CCB separately.

By mail

  1. Fill out and sign Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application.
  2. Mail the form to your tax center.
  3. If any of the following situations apply, you must also provide supporting documents with your application:

    4. Male parent is primarily responsible for the child

    If the male parent is primarily responsible, you must:

    1. Include a letter from the female parent stating that the male parent is primarily responsible for the care of all the children in the household.
    2. Mail the letter and Form RC66 to your tax centre.

    A letter is not required if both parents living with the child are of the same sex.

    You are requesting benefits for a child that was born outside Canada or that was born in Canada and is more than one year old:

    You must provide proof of birth with your application if the CRA has never paid benefits for this child

    1. Provide proof of birth
    2. Mail the proof of birth and Form RC66 to your tax centre.
    You are applying for a period that started more than 11 months ago

    If you are applying for a child that started living with you more than 11 months ago, you must:

    1. Provide supporting documents for
      1. Your citizenship status (1 of the following)
        • Canadian birth certificate
        • Canadian passport
        • permanent residence card
        • immigration status in Canada for you and your spouse or common-law partner
      2. Proof that you live in Canada (at least 3 different documents type)
        • lease agreement
        • rent receipt
        • household bill (gas, electricity, cable television, telephone)
        • driver's license, vehicle registration, or car insurance
        • membership in social or professional organizations
      3. Proof of birth for each child you are applying for
      4. Proof that you are primarily responsible for the care and upbringing of the child (at least 3 different document types):
        • a letter from the nursery or school authority showing the contact information on file for the child
        • a copy of the child's report card, showing the contact information on file for the child
        • a copy of the child's school registration or enrolment document, or information or emergency contact sheet, signed and certified by the school to be a true copy
        • if your child is being home-schooled, a letter from the school board or appropriate educational authority confirming that you have a home school arrangement
        • a registration form or a receipt from an activity or club the child was enrolled in
        • a complete and signed court order, decree, or separation agreement; the document must explain the type of custody arrangement you have (for example, shared custody) and clearly show the living arrangements for the child
        • any other document showing that the child lived with you during the period being reviewed
    2. Mail all supporting documents and Form RC66 to your tax centre.
    You or your spouse or common-law partner are newcomers or returning residents to Canada

    If you are:

    • a newcomer or returned as a resident of Canada in the last two years
    • a new Canadian citizens within the last 12 months
    • a permanent resident
    • a protected person (refugee)
    • a temporary resident who has lived in Canada for the previous 18 months
    • not a Canadian citizen, but are an Indian within the meaning of the Indian Act

    You must:

    1. Fill out and sign Schedule RC66SCH, Status in Canada/Statement of Income
    2. Mail Schedule RC66SCH and Form RC66 to your tax centre.
